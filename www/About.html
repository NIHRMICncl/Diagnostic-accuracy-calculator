This is the first of a collection of interactive Shiny apps that allow you to calculate diagnostic accuracy statistics and explore the intricacies of evaluating and using diagnostic tests:
  •  Diagnostic accuracy calculator.  App to calculate the main diagnostic accuracy statistics.
  • Sample size planner. Interactive app for estimating sample sizes for diagnostic accuracy studies.
  • Interpreting diagnostic accuracy measures. Interactive app to explore the main measures of diagnostic accuracy: sensitivity and specificity (technically useful) and positive and negative predictive values (clinically useful )  (under construction).
  • Interpreting ROC curves. Interactive app to explore the trade offs between high sensitivity and high specificity when selecting the classification threshold for a test with a continuous result. (under construction).
  • Interpreting the clinical utility of diagnostic tests. Interactive app to explore how to take account of the uncertainties in test results and the values placed on the range of possible outcomes (benefits, harms, costs) when making clinical decisions. (under construction).
  • Interpreting evaluations with an imperfect reference test. cto explore the uncertainties that arise in evaluating a diagnostic test when there is no gold standard to compare its performance. (under construction).
  • Interpreting epidemic transmissibility measures. Interactive app to explore the effects of the infection's transmissibility and the test's accuracy on the course of an epidemic. (under construction).
You can use this app to:
  • Calculate diagnostic accuracy measures
  • See how predictive values vary with prevalence
  • See the uncertainties in positive and negative predictive values
